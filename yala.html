YALA NATIONAL PARK


Yala is Sri Lanka's most famous national park. yala was designated a wild life sanctuary in 1990 and was designated a national park in 1938.yala national park plays a very significiant role in the conservation of a large number of flora and fauna species in Sri Lanka, many of which are endemic to the island. Historical and religious sites such as Katharaama, Sithulpawwa and the magul maha vihara and many archecologically accommodation options in yala national park.Sri lankka important places add additonal significance to the area. The safari goers will be enthralled when visiting yala national park that is not only renowned for its wildlife but also noted for its rich history.

yala national park safari apart from tracking leopards , the yala National Park is home to a variety of game, both big and small that will keep a family of a wildlife enthusiats on safari , busy. there is a substantial Ari Lankan Elephant population at the Yala National Park, which is a recognized subspecies of the Asian Elephant, and can often be spotted in large herds, baby elephants in tow, at various locations inside the National park. The other 44 species of mammals at yala National Park include the Sri Lankan Sloth Bear, Wild Water Buffaloes, Spotted Deer and many others. Along with the leopard , some of these are threatened species as well.

The Yala National Park is incredibly diverse when it comes to ecosystems.these include moist monsoon forests, dry monsoon forests, semi deciduous forests, thorn forests, grasslands, fresh water and marine wetlands and beaches. The Yala National Park was one of the first two National Parks to be established in Sril Lanka.
